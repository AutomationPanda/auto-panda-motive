While replacing the rear brakes, I discovered that their dust shields were thoroughly rusted.
The parking brake springs even came out of the passenger side.
I had little choice but to replace them.
Unfortunately, to replace the dust shields on the W203 the _right_ way,
the wheel hub must be removed – which effectively destroys the wheel bearings in the process.

So, I did it all.
The bearings were original and should have been replaced anyway.
I disassembled both rear wheels, pressed in new bearings, and installed fresh dust shields.
While the wheel was disassembled, I replaced the rear shocks and springs as well.
The shocks were pretty weak, and the bottoms of the springs were pitted with rust.
