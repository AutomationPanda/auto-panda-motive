After dropping the rear subframe, I stripped it down to the differential and the axles.
Then, I lifted the subframe off the differential, put the new one in its place, and reconnected all the parts.
I used this opportunity to replace all the suspension arms and the sway bar, too.
The rust was _deep_.
I could poke my finger straight through parts of the subframe to make it crumble.
One of the suspension arm mounts literally fell off.

After reinstalling the rear subframe, I noticed that the wheels had severe toe-in.
It turned out that one of the suspension control arms was an inch too short!
That's what I get for buying aftermarket parts.
I bought an OEM part to replace it, and it fit perfectly.
When it was all said and done, the car was back on the road
after being out of commission for more than a month.
