This car was built in 2006.
Its stock radio unit was old-school:
it had AM, FM, and a 6-CD changer.
Its display had only digital letters and numbers.
It certainly didn't have USB ports,
and it didn't even have audio jack inputs.
Surprisingly, though, it had Bluetooth for phone calls.

One of the best upgrades I made was installing a new touchscreen radio unit.
I found one on eBay with the right form factor to look seamless.
Installing it was a bit challenging:
I connected one of the wires improperly at first, and the unit wouldn't turn on.
Once it was in, it was great!
Connecting my phone with Apple CarPlay to play music and navigate with GPS
makes the car feel like it belongs in 2026.
