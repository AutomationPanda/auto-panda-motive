This was the day that my mom gifted me this car.
I flew up to Baltimore to speak at the DevOps Days Baltimore event.
Then, my mom signed the title over to me, and I drove it home to NC.
On the way, I even stopped in DC to visit one of my best friends.

Unfortunately, I didn't quite make it home that night.
At about 10pm, while driving 70mph on I-85 S near Alberta, Virginia,
I felt a big "boom" as if I had hit a big pothole.
The car started fishtailing violently.
Thankfully, there was an exit nearby with a well-lit gas station.
I had to hold the steering wheel 90º to the right to keep it moving straight.
When I parked, I noticed that the rear driver-side wheel had severe negative camber and toe-out.
I could not determine the reason for the failure, but I knew I could not drive it further.

I called AAA to tow me home.
Thankfully, Uncle Jackson's shop was within the 100-mile towing limit.
The tow truck arrived at 2:30am, and we arrived at the shop at about 4am.
Uncle Jackson met me there upon arrival.
When we lifted up the car on the jack,
we quickly discovered that the rear subframe had rusted to the point of cracking.

We knew the doors had rust, but we didn't know that the underbelly had rusted, too.
It was only a matter of time before the subframe cracked.
Apparently, rear subframe rust is a known issue with C-class models from the 2000s-2010s.
I sought a goodwill repair from Mercedes-Benz, but they rejected my request.
Thankfully, I survived this incident without a scratch – 
and I had the skills and the means to make the repairs myself (with some help from Uncle Jackson).
