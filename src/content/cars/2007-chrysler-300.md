The Chrysler 300 was the first car I owned for myself.
I bought it in 2010 after graduating college.
I paid $17K for it in full with money saved from my summer internships at IBM.
At the time, it was 3 years old and had 25K miles on it.

I _dearly_ loved this car.
It was so much nicer than I ever deserved.
It offered a comfortable ride, a spacious interior, and a nice sound system.
One of my favorite parts was how the dashboard lit up blue-green at night.
I will never forget its unique smell, too – one that I only ever found again in Uncle Jackson's 2007 Mercedes-Benz E350.
This Chrysler served as a workhorse: driving me up and down the East Coast.
There were times it went to the beach.
There were times I jammed it full of IKEA furniture.
There were even a few minor fender-benders.

Ultimately, I let this car go in January 2021.
After 11 years and 150K miles, it had a leak around one of its cylinders.
At this time, I was working from home due to COVID, and my wife and I didn't need two cars.
I sold it to Carmax for $1000.

I sorely regret selling this car.
If I had the skills and tools I have now, then I would have fixed it myself.
However, back then, I had neither.
Nevertheless, selling the Chrysler 300 opened the way for me to buy the [1970 VW Beetle](/cars/1970-vw-beetle/),
which in turn forced me to start working on cars myself – and truly turned me into a "car guy."
